About signal noise

Signal noise models situations where players cannot perfectly observe each other's behavior — due to miscommunication, ambiguous actions, or imperfect information. Even a small error rate (5–10%) can cause tit-for-tat to fall into extended punishment cycles: one corrupted signal triggers retaliation, which triggers more retaliation, and neither player knows why the other "suddenly" defected.

More forgiving strategies — like tit-for-two-tats, which only retaliates after two consecutive perceived defections — tend to be more robust to noise. Try designing a memory-2 or memory-3 strategy that handles noise better than the presets.
